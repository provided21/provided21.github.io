Over the past semester, I've been building a comprehensive knowledge graph covering 14 areas of digital marketing communication. What started as a simple note-taking exercise evolved into something much more interesting: a structured, queryable map of how concepts in digital communication connect to each other.

## The Framework

The knowledge base is organized around three layers:

1. **Chapter Notes (14 topics)** — from search marketing and e-commerce to AI marketing and data privacy
2. **Concept Notes (19 entries)** — deep dives into specific mechanisms like RFM models, recommendation systems, and private domain traffic
3. **Mechanism Notes** — cross-cutting themes that span multiple chapters, like "Cookie deprecation → first-party data rise"

## Human-AI Collaboration

One design principle I stuck to: **the human perspective leads, AI supports**. Each note follows a format where:

- 🙋 **My questions** drive the exploration
- 🤖 **AI responses** provide technical depth
- 📎 **Cross-references** link related concepts

This isn't about letting AI write the knowledge base — it's about using AI as a thought partner to probe deeper into concepts I'm genuinely curious about.

## The Graph View

The most satisfying part is watching the Obsidian graph view grow organically over time. What began as disconnected notes gradually formed clusters, then bridges between clusters. The visual representation makes it obvious which concepts are central hubs and which are peripheral — a kind of intellectual cartography.

## Tools Used

- **Obsidian** for note-taking and graph visualization
- **Mermaid.js** for inline diagrams
- **Claude** as an AI discussion partner
- **Python** for data processing and analysis
